Os fundamentos da República Federativa do Brasil estão estabelecidos principalmente na Constituição de 1988, e podem ser resumidos nos seguintes pontos:

*   **Soberania:** O Brasil é um Estado soberano, ou seja, independente e não subordinado a nenhum outro país.
*   **Cidadania:** A Constituição assegura direitos e deveres aos cidadãos brasileiros, garantindo a participação na vida política e social do país.

*   **Federalismo:** O Brasil é uma união de estados autônomos, com governo próprio, mas integrados em um único país.
*   **Separação de Poderes:** Os poderes Executivo, Legislativo e Judiciário são independentes e harmônicos entre si, com funções específicas para garantir o equilíbrio e o funcionamento do Estado.
*   **Busca pelo Bem Comum:** O Estado deve buscar o bem-estar geral da população, promovendo a justiça social e a redução das desigualdades.

Esses fundamentos formam a base da organização política e social do Brasil, definindo seus princípios e valores essenciais.

## Main Lines of Evidence That Humans Are Causing Climate Change:

The scientific consensus is overwhelmingly that humans are the primary driver of current climate change. This isn't based on a single piece of evidence, but a convergence of multiple independent lines of evidence. Here are the main ones:

**1. Increased Greenhouse Gas Concentrations:**

* **Direct Measurement:**  We directly measure increasing concentrations of greenhouse gases (GHGs) like carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O) in the atmosphere. These measurements, taken globally for decades (e.g., Mauna Loa Observatory for CO2 since 1958), show a clear and accelerating upward trend.
* **Source Identification (Isotope Analysis):**  Scientists can analyze the *isotopes* (different forms of an element) of carbon in atmospheric CO2. This "fingerprinting" reveals that the excess CO2 comes from burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas), which release carbon that's been locked underground for millions of years.  Fossil fuels have a distinct isotopic signature different from natural sources like volcanoes or respiration.
* **Correlation with Human Activities:** The increase in GHG concentrations coincides *precisely* with the Industrial Revolution and the massive increase in fossil fuel combustion, deforestation, and certain agricultural practices.

**2. Warming Temperatures:**

* **Global Temperature Rise:**  Global average temperatures have risen significantly over the past century, particularly in recent decades. Multiple independent datasets (NASA GISS, NOAA NCEI, HadCRUT) all show this warming trend. 2023 was the warmest year on record, and each of the last several years have been among the warmest ever recorded.
* **Ocean Warming:** The oceans have absorbed over 90% of the excess heat trapped by GHGs.  Ocean temperature measurements show a clear warming trend throughout the water column. 
* **Warming Trends are Uneven:**  The Arctic is warming at a rate two to four times faster than the global average – a pattern consistent with climate models predicting amplified warming in polar regions.
* **Reduced Cooling in Upper Atmosphere:**  If the warming were due to solar variations, we'd expect to see warming throughout the atmosphere. However, the upper atmosphere is actually *cooling*, indicating that heat is being trapped in the lower atmosphere by GHGs.

**3. Other Climate Changes Consistent with GHG Forcing:**

* **Melting Ice:** Glaciers and ice sheets (Greenland, Antarctica) are losing mass at an accelerating rate.  Arctic sea ice extent has dramatically declined. This melting contributes to sea level rise.
* **Sea Level Rise:** Sea levels are rising due to both thermal expansion (water expands as it warms) and the addition of water from melting glaciers and ice sheets.
* **Ocean Acidification:**  The ocean absorbs about 30% of the CO2 released into the atmosphere. This absorption lowers the pH of ocean water, leading to ocean acidification, which threatens marine ecosystems.
* **Changes in Precipitation Patterns:** Some regions are experiencing more intense rainfall and flooding, while others are facing more frequent and severe droughts. These changes are consistent with the expected effects of a warming climate.
* **Increased Frequency of Extreme Weather Events:** While attributing individual events to climate change is complex, there’s increasing evidence that climate change is increasing the frequency and/or intensity of certain extreme weather events, such as heatwaves, heavy precipitation, and wildfires.

**4. Climate Models:**

* **Successful Reproductions of Past Climate:** Climate models, based on the laws of physics, can accurately reproduce observed climate patterns from the past when incorporating the effects of human-caused GHG emissions.
* **Projections of Future Climate:** These models are used to project future climate change scenarios. They consistently show that continued GHG emissions will lead to further warming and other climate changes.
* **Attribution Studies:** Climate models allow scientists to run simulations *with* and *without* human-caused GHG emissions. The difference between the two scenarios demonstrates the substantial impact of human activities on the climate.



**In summary:** It's not just *one* piece of evidence, but the *combination* of these observations, coupled with the underlying physics and sophisticated climate modeling, that provides a robust and compelling case for human-caused climate change.

### Query: What are the main lines of evidence that humans are causing climate change?
**Source**: https://royalsociety.org/-/media/Royal_Society_Content/policy/projects/climate-evidence-causes/climate-change-evidence-causes.pdf

**Answer**:
The scientific consensus is that humans are the primary cause of global warming and climate change. This conclusion is based on a convergence of evidence from multiple independent fields of science, including physics, chemistry, biology, and meteorology.

Here are the main lines of evidence that support this conclusion:

### 1. Rising Concentrations of Greenhouse Gases
The atmosphere contains gases that trap heat, known as greenhouse gases (GHGs). Measurements of the atmosphere show that the concentration of CO2, methane, and nitrous oxide has risen significantly since the beginning of the Industrial Revolution.
*   **The Carbon Dioxide Link:** The amount of CO2 in the atmosphere is now higher than it has been in at least 800,000 years. The vast majority of this excess carbon can be traced directly to the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas) and deforestation.
*   **The Carbon Cycle:** The carbon cycle describes the flow of carbon through the Earth's systems. Human activities have fundamentally broken this cycle by removing carbon from underground (fossil fuels) and releasing it into the atmosphere, where it cannot be absorbed quickly enough.

### 2. Isotope Analysis (The "Smoking Gun")
While scientists knew that CO2 levels were rising, they needed to prove *who* or *what* put it there.
*   **Fossil Fuel Signature:** Carbon atoms exist in different forms (isotopes). Fossil fuels (ancient plant and animal matter) have a distinct isotopic signature (specifically, they are depleted in Carbon-13) compared to the carbon in the atmosphere.
*   **The Proof:** Measurements of the rising CO2 show exactly the same isotopic "fingerprint" as the fossil fuels we burn. Natural sources like volcanoes or decomposition produce a different signature, ruling them out as the primary cause.

### 3. Changes in the Ocean
The oceans absorb about 90% of the excess heat generated by greenhouse gases and about 30% of the carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere.
*   **Ocean Warming:** Heat content measurements show that the ocean is warming rapidly, particularly at the surface.
*   **Ocean Acidification:** As the ocean absorbs CO2, it reacts chemically to become more acidic. The pH of the ocean has dropped significantly, which poses a threat to marine ecosystems like coral reefs and shellfish.

### 4. Physical Changes in the Earth System
The warming atmosphere is causing physical changes across the planet that match the predictions of climate models:
*   **Melting Ice:** There is widespread melting of mountain glaciers, Greenland’s ice sheet, and Antarctic ice. This contributes to rising sea levels.
*   **Sea Level Rise:** Global mean sea level has risen about 8 inches since 1880.
*   **Permafrost Thaw:** Large areas of permanently frozen ground in the Arctic are thawing, releasing more methane (a potent greenhouse gas) and altering local ecosystems.

### 5. Climate Modeling
Scientists use supercomputers to create complex computer models that simulate the Earth's climate. To test these models, they run simulations of past climate conditions.
*   **The Human Factor:** Models that include natural factors (like volcanic eruptions and solar changes) and *human* factors (like fossil fuel emissions) accurately reproduce the temperature trends we have observed over the last century.
*   **The Failure:** Models that exclude human factors fail to match the actual temperature record, proving that natural causes alone cannot explain the rapid warming.

### 6. The Rule Out of Natural Factors
To ensure the warming wasn't caused by the sun or other natural forces, scientists have checked the following:
*   **Solar Irradiance:** Satellite data shows that the sun’s energy output has actually remained stable or dipped slightly over the last 50 years, yet temperatures have risen. This rules out the sun as the primary driver.
*   **Volcanic Activity:** Large volcanic eruptions cause short-term cooling by spewing ash into the stratosphere, but the global temperature trend since the 1980s shows no such cooling pattern.
